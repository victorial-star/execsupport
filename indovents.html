Indorama Ventures (IVL) is a Bangkok-headquartered, SET-listed global chemicals and fibers conglomerate founded in 1994 by CEO Aloke Lohia. They are the world's largest producer of PET resin — 1 in every 5 PET bottles on earth is made from their resin. Their US business spans four segments: Combined PET (CPET), Indovinya (specialty chemicals/surfactants), Fibers (FiberVisions bicomponent fibers + Avgol nonwovens), and Indovida (packaging). The US is one of their most strategically important markets, anchored by massive integrated manufacturing clusters in Alabama, Texas, South Carolina, Georgia, and North Carolina.

Methods of Import & Export
  • Ocean FCL (40' Dry): Primary method for PET resin pellets (supersacks/bags), PTA, polyolefin resins, fiber raw materials. Main trade lanes: Houston ↔ Antwerp, Singapore, Kaohsiung, Rotterdam.
  • Bulk Liquid Chemical Tankers: MTBE, MEG, EO, LAB at Port Neches and Clear Lake TX. Moves via marine terminals — not container drayage.
  • Flexitanks: Used for glycols and liquid chemical container shipments where applicable.
  • Rail (NS / CSX): Alabama facilities heavily served by rail for bulk resin. PET shipped in hopper cars. Important for Decatur, AL cluster.
  • OTR Trucking: Regional distribution of PET resin to beverage/packaging customers. Brandon Bost owns OTR + 3PL category.
  • Pipeline: Ethylene from Westlake, LA to Clear Lake TX and Port Neches TX. No container drayage relevance.
Drayage Sweet Spot: Container moves from port to plant gate — paraxylene and PTA imports into Mobile → Decatur; MEG and polyolefin imports via Savannah → GA fiber plants; and PP imports via Wilmington/Charlotte → Mocksville, NC (Avgol).
Technology: IVL runs SAP S/4HANA ERP with SAP Taulia supply chain finance (launched Aug 2024). They are a sophisticated logistics buyer — expect data-driven conversations about on-time performance, exceptions, and TMS integration capability.
Major News & Strategic Context (Last 18 Months)
IVL 2.0 → SOAR Restructuring (2025–2028): IVL declared 2025 a "year of reset" after EBITDA fell ~35% YoY amid global petrochemical overcapacity, destocking, and volatile crude prices. They announced IVL 2.0 in March 2025 — shifting from M&A growth to cost leadership and portfolio optimization. $200M+ in fixed cost savings targeted; sites rationalized in Portugal, Netherlands, Australia, Canada. New 3-year plan (SOAR 2026–2028) targets doubling EBITDA by 2028. Translation: Every vendor — including drayage — is under cost scrutiny. But reliable, multi-lane partners who reduce friction are highly valuable right now.
US PET Tariffs Through October 2026: US reciprocal tariffs at 20% on PET imports protect domestic IVL plants from cheap Asian competition. IVL estimates $20–25M/year positive impact. US plant utilization is likely increasing — meaning more container moves at US ports.
Avgol Mocksville, NC — $100M New Line (Feb 2025): Brand new high-speed spunbond nonwoven production line opened February 17, 2025. "Made in America" positioning. Polypropylene raw materials need to move in via container. Their drayage relationships at this facility may still be forming — this is a fresh opportunity.
Planned Spin-offs — Indovinya & Indovida: IVL is carving out its specialty chemicals (Indovinya — TX plants) and packaging (Indovida) businesses as independent entities with their own IPOs. Logistics contracts may be up for renegotiation as these become standalone companies. A transition window for new drayage relationships.
Corpus Christi Polymers (CCP) — Still Paused: The planned 1.1M MT/year PET + 1.3M MT/year PTA plant in Corpus Christi (JV with Alpek and Far Eastern) has been paused since Sept 2023 due to cost inflation and high interest rates. Partners are evaluating phased completion. When/if it resumes, this would be a transformational drayage opportunity at Port of Corpus Christi.
